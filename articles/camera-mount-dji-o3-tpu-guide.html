Best 3D Printed Camera Mounts for DJI O3 & Walksnail: Angle, Material & Fit (2026)

Apr 1, 2026 8 Min Read Updated Apr 2026

A camera mount that does not hold its angle under G-forces, vibrates at hover, or cracks on the second crash is worse than useless. We tested 12 community designs across three materials and three camera angles and found four that we would actually trust.

What We Evaluated

Every design was printed in Shore 95A TPU (for vibration isolation) and PETG (for rigidity comparison) and tested for: camera unit fit precision (no rattle, no need for foam padding), angle retention under 5G simulated manoeuvres on a jig, surface finish on the camera-contact surfaces, and crash survival in 10 drop tests from 1.5 metres onto concrete.

The best mounts used TPU for vibration isolation but maintained tight tolerances — looser than PETG equivalent tolerances to account for Shore hardness compression. This is the key tuning variable in camera mount design.

DJI O3 30° TPU Mount by Mellow_FPV Best Overall

This community design from Printables.com is the best DJI O3 mount we tested. The 30° wedge angle suits most FPV freestyle flying styles, and the TPU geometry — specifically the recessed camera pocket with compression-fit sides — holds the O3 unit with zero rattle at full vibration while providing genuine impact absorption. Printed in 95A TPU with 4 perimeters and 60% gyroid infill.

O3 25° PETG Rigid Mount Low Angle Pick

For racing and technical flying where precise horizon reference matters more than vibration isolation, a rigid PETG 25° mount is preferable. The lower angle gives a cleaner horizon for gate threading. The PETG formulation we recommend is Bambu Lab PETG — consistent, stiff, and dimensionally accurate enough to hold O3 unit tolerances without play.

Print Settings for Camera Mounts

For TPU mounts: direct drive extruder required, 20–25 mm/s print speed, 4 perimeters, 60% gyroid infill, 220°C. For PETG mounts: standard settings, 4+ perimeters, 40% infill minimum, 230°C. The most critical dimension to get right is the camera unit pocket — print a single-wall test ring of the camera slot before committing to a full mount.
