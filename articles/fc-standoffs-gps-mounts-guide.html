FC Standoffs, GPS Masts & Antenna Mounts: The 3D Print Guide for FPV Builds (2026)

Apr 1, 2026 7 Min Read Updated Apr 2026

Three of the highest-value printable parts on an FPV drone are not the glamorous ones: FC standoffs, GPS masts, and antenna tubes. Get them right and your electronics are protected, your GPS signal is clean, and your gyro noise floor is lower. Here is the complete guide.

Flight Controller Standoffs

Stock M3 aluminium standoffs are rigid — they transmit every motor vibration directly into your FC gyroscope. Replacing them with printed TPU standoffs is one of the most impactful single changes you can make to your tune. Shore 95A TPU at 8–10mm height is the standard for most 5-inch builds: soft enough to absorb motor vibration, stiff enough to maintain stack geometry.

Print at 30.5mm inner spacing (standard stack dimension) with M3 clearance holes at 3.3mm diameter to account for TPU compression. 4 perimeters, 100% infill for standoffs — you want solid material for load-bearing components.

GPS Mast

A GPS mast lifts your GPS module 40–60mm above the main electronics — critical for EMI isolation from ESCs and motors, which produce significant electrical interference at the frequencies GPS receivers use. A properly positioned GPS module can cut time-to-first-fix from 90 seconds to under 15. Print your GPS mast in PETG or ASA — stiff material, no flexing under G-forces.

The key design parameter: route the GPS cable inside the mast tube if possible — it acts as a shielded conduit. Use M3 hardware at the base with vibration-isolating rubber o-rings between the mast foot and the frame plate.

Antenna Protection Tubes

ELRS and TBS Crossfire receiver antennas are fragile — bare wire antennas get snagged, bent, or broken in crashes. Printed ASA antenna tubes protect the antenna wire and maintain the critical 90° orientation that maximises radio signal reception. Print in ASA for UV stability — antenna tubes live in the most UV-exposed position on the frame.

Material Quick Reference

FC standoffs: TPU 95A for vibration isolation, 100% infill. GPS mast: PETG or ASA for rigidity, 40%+ infill. Antenna tubes: ASA for UV stability, 100% infill on tube walls. All three parts benefit from 4+ perimeters regardless of infill — perimeter count drives the strength of small printed parts more than infill does.
